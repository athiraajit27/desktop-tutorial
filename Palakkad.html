Palakkad

Celebrated as the Granary of Kerala, Palakkad is a vast stretch of fertile plains interspersed with hills, rivers, mountain streams and forests. A potpourri of Tamil and Kerala culture, some of the finest Carnatic musicians hail from this district which continues to be a principally agrarian society.

Parambikulam Tiger Reserve

The Parambikulam Tiger Reserve, a rich swathe of greenery brimming with wildlife, is a 285 square kilometer protected area cuddled in Palakkad.Like an oasis of remarkable serenity, located approximately 110 kilometers from Palakkad district, Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is spot of exotic variety of flora and fauna.

Nelliyampathy Hills

Nelliyampathy, often known as 'poor man's Ooty' is a favourite among travellers for its evergreen forests.This hilly village is the delight of trekkers. It beckons visitors with Shola forests and grasslands in the Western Ghats adjacent to Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary.

Malampuzha Garden & Dam

Malampuzha Gardens and the Irrigation Dam tend to be the areas that attract the highest number of visitors.Malampuzha is the only rock-cut garden in South India made by Nek Chand, the renowned artist and winner of the prestigious Padmashree Award.
